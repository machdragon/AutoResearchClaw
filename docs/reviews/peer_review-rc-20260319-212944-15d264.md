# Stage 18: Peer Review

### Reviewer A (Methodology Expert)

#### Strengths
- Clear, well-motivated methodological goal: align training with the fixed int8+zlib channel and Parameter Golf lane_3 constraints (val_bpb_roundtrip, 16MB cap, runtime ≤ 1.10× baseline).
- Method section provides precise mathematical formalization of the objective, roundtrip operator, and staged QAT, including notation for float vs roundtrip metrics.
- Good conceptual design of staged QAT and the H1 entropy-regularization extension, with attention to where in the architecture fake quantization and proxies are applied.
- The paper is unusually honest about the evolution from flawed initial experiments to a more infrastructure-aware harness with wiring tests and gate logic.

#### Weaknesses

1. **Severe divergence between claimed experiments and actual code/execution**
   - The paper describes three conditions (BL, QAT, H1), multi-run harness improvements, and numerous metrics (val_bpb, val_bpb_roundtrip, runtime_ms, artifact_bytes, promotion flags, etc.).
   - The **actual main.py** only implements a single “QAT_only” condition, with:
     - `condition = "QAT_only"`
     - Fixed hyperparameters (QAT enabled, compression_reg_lambda = 0.0).
     - A stub `run_condition()` that always raises `RuntimeError`, because subprocess execution is disabled.
   - The **actual run result (run-1.json)** shows:
     - No metrics reported (`"metrics": {}`).
     - A crash before even instantiating the harness: `ModuleNotFoundError: No module named 'harness'`.
   - Therefore:
     - No candidate_bpb or candidate_runtime_ms were ever computed.
     - No gates were evaluated in practice.
     - The detailed numerical results given in the Method/Experiments/Results sections (artifact_bytes, runtime_ms, etc.) are **fabricated or at best hypothetical**, not backed by executable code in this environment.
     - There is only **1 failed trial**; the paper implies full runs for BL, QAT, H1.

2. **Topic alignment and scope creep**
   - Overall, the paper stays conceptually on topic: Parameter Golf lane_3, val_bpb_roundtrip minimization, staged QAT aligned with int8+zlib, and budget constraints.
   - However, the text substantially drifts into:
     - Broad discussions of benchmarking health, carbon impact, radiology, multi-modal benchmarks, etc., which are **not directly contributing methods or experiments** for lane_3 and inflate the narrative.
     - Multiple paragraphs about benchmark ethics and sustainability that would be more appropriate in a short discussion paragraph, not as central contributions.
   - Some contributions (e.g., “We develop a reproducible, budget-aware experimental harness…”) are not actually realized in the provided code: the harness is missing, and the run fails due to `ModuleNotFoundError: harness`.

3. **Claim–evidence misalignment (major)**
   - Title claim: “Teaching Language Models the Compression Channel in Parameter Golf.”
     - Implies demonstrable adaptation to the compression channel in a Parameter Golf setting.
     - Evidence: no successful runs, no measurement of val_bpb_roundtrip; main.py cannot even import the harness.
     - This is **unsupported experimentally**.
   - Abstract and conclusion claims:
     - “We obtain robust multi-run measurements…”
       - Actual trial count: **1** (failed). No multi-run evidence.
     - “We show that compression-channel-aware QAT can match or improve post-roundtrip bits-per-byte within the same artifact and runtime budgets.”
       - No support: code never reaches evaluation; we have no actual `val_bpb_roundtrip` numbers, nor artifact_bytes, nor runtime_ms from a successful run.
       - The text claims H1 passes quick-gate despite runtime_ratio > 1.10, contradicting its own budget statement.
     - “We provide an empirical study showing that…”
       - There is no empirical study in the sense of executed, recorded experiments.
   - Many specific numeric claims (e.g., BL vs QAT vs H1 val_bpb, artifact_bytes = 8M vs 4.8M, runtime_ms ~63k–64k, spreads of 0.045 etc.) are **not backed by the actual experiment logs** (which are empty due to crash).
   - Tables and LaTeX figures in Results:
     - All metrics (means, stds, spreads) are not traceable to the provided evidence; they appear to be hand-constructed hypotheticals.

4. **Completeness and structure**
   - Word counts:
     - Introduction ~1337 words (well above 800–1000 target).
     - Related Work ~1062 words (above 600–800).
     - Method ~2583 words (well over the 1000–1500 target).
     - Experiments ~1648 words; Results ~1175 words (both above targets).
     - Conclusion very short (~135 words vs 200–300 target).
     - Discussion is relatively short vs Method (ratio ~3.8×).
   - While the paper is long enough for NeurIPS, it is **imbalanced**, devoting excessive space to methodology exposition and related work vs concise, solid experimental evidence.
