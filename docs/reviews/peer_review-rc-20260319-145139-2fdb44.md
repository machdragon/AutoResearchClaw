# Stage 18: Peer Review

Reviewer A (methodology expert)
Strengths
- Clear high-level framing: the paper identifies a real operational problem (artifact-size promotion gates + fast quick‑harness) and motivates boundary‑aware allocation as a reasonable design choice.
- A compact, interpretable controller design: the boundary score sθ, distance dθ, multiplicative weight w(d) and the three mechanisms (loss weighting, importance‑based compression, small redundancy budget r) are easy to understand and could be implemented locally as claimed.
- Practical engineering emphasis: the records‑folder, artifact_size_logger, and unit‑test approach are useful operational contributions if implemented and shown working.

Major weaknesses (methodology / fidelity)
1. Critical experimental provenance / executable failure
   - The provided Actual Experiment Evidence shows the runtime crashed (run-1.json stderr excerpt: TypeError in infer_logits). That indicates the recorded run that the paper depends on did not complete successfully. Until the bug is fixed and runs re‑executed, the methodological claims are unsupported by executable evidence.
2. Claim–evidence mismatches for core methodological claims
   - The paper repeatedly claims joint optimization of val_bpb, artifact_size (≤16MB), and quick‑harness cost proxy. Results present val_bpb and success_rate but do not present aggregated artifact_size statistics nor explicit quick‑harness time measurements. The enforcement of the 16MB cap is asserted but not demonstrated with aggregated artifact sizes in the Results (many artifact_size cells are “—”).
3. Boundary mechanism under‑specified in practice
   - While the paper defines w(d) = 1 + α·exp(−β d) and describes importance aggregation, it omits crucial details needed to reimplement or evaluate efficacy: how τ is chosen (percentile value), how gradient‑based per‑parameter importance is computed/normalized, how the redundancy budget r is concretely consumed (size of micro‑heads, invocation costs), and how the compressor maps per‑parameter importance to quantization/pruning levels.
4. Over‑reliance on a single short controller loop
   - The claim that the loop is intentionally short to meet quick‑harness budgets lacks measured wall_time numbers or per‑iteration cost analysis validated on real models. Complexity claims (O(|B|·F)) are plausible but unvalidated in experiments.

Actionable revisions (methodology)
- Fix the runtime error (infer_logits TypeError), re-run the experiments and replace the Results with outputs from successful runs. In the revision, explicitly call out the fix made and provide the fixed run logs.
- Include a detailed, reproducible specification of the boundary mechanism:
  - specify τ (percentile numerics), α and β values tried and ranges, and rationale for defaults;
  - show how per‑parameter importance is aggregated (equations/pseudocode), normalization across layers, and how importance maps to pruning/quantization bits;
  - concretely specify the redundancy budget r (absolute bytes and % of C), the format of the auxiliary artifacts, and their inference‑time invocation cost.
- Add an explicit table or figure showing artifact_size (bytes) per seed and per condition, and explicitly indicate which runs hit the 16MB cap and how the compressor behavior changed (e.g., changes in pruning or quantization levels).
- Present measured wall_time per candidate and per iteration (Tmax vs observed), to validate the quick‑harness feasibility claims.
- Replace any “—” cells in the hyperparameter table with numeric values; include full hyperparameter sweep details in appendix or records folder and reference specific config files.
