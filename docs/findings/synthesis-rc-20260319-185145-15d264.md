## Cluster 1 – Training-only strategies to improve deployment-time efficiency

**Representative work:** `zhang2020training`

**Key ideas**

- Uses a fixed, lightweight inference backbone (ResNet-18) and improves accuracy solely via **training-time tricks**:
  - Multi-size input training (scale robustness).
  - Auxiliary triplet-loss branch (better metric structure).
  - Dropout between feature extractor and classifier.
- All added branches/operations exist **only during training**; the **inference graph and cost are unchanged**.

**Relevance to lane_3 (QAT + roundtrip-aware compression)**

- Direct conceptual analogue:
  - Your lane: introduce **quantization-aware training and compression simulation** only during training; keep final runtime model within **runtime ≤1.10×** and **artifact ≤16MB**.
  - `zhang2020training`: introduce extra losses and branches only during training; deployed model has the **same compute and parameters** as baseline.
- Their methodology suggests:
  - You can aggressively augment the training graph (e.g., fake-quant ops, zlib roundtrip simulation, auxiliary heads for compression robustness) without violating the quick-gate, as long as these are pruned for inference.
  - Multi-size training parallels **exposing the model to different quantization / compression regimes** so it becomes robust to varying bit-width or compression quality (analogous to “multi-size images” → “multi-precision / multi-compression”).

**Clustered techniques you can borrow**

- **Auxiliary losses that vanish at inference:**
  - Triplet-like loss on pre-quant and post-roundtrip embeddings to force **compression-invariant representations**.
- **Training-time data/transform diversity:**
  - Analogous to multi-size images, sample:
    - Different quantization schemes (int8 symmetric/asymmetric, per-channel vs per-tensor).
    - Different compression levels or zlib configs.
  - This helps the model generalize to the exact deployed quantization+zlib combination with minimal val_bpb degradation.

## Cluster 2 – Self-distillation and representation shaping under label constraints

**Representative work:** `zhang2021rssskd`

**Key ideas**

- RS-SSKD uses:
  - A **two-branch network** with augmented views (original + transformed) and CAM guidance to push attention to category-relevant regions.
  - **Self-knowledge distillation** within one architecture: a “teacher” path regularizes a “student” path.
- Emphasis is on **representation quality** under **data scarcity**, using **additional training-only structure and losses**; the deployed model stays compact.

**Relevance to lane_3**

- The pattern is almost exactly what you want:
  - Two-branch / multi-branch architectures at training to create **teacher/student or clean/roundtrip views**.
  - Distillation losses that make compressed/quantized outputs mimic full-precision behavior.
