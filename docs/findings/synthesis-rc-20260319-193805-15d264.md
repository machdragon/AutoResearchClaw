## Cluster 1 – QAT Efficiency & Data/Parameter Reduction

**Papers:** huang2023efficient (ACS), ke2025dlqat (DL-QAT)

**Common ideas**

- **Selective training effort instead of full-dataset, full-parameter QAT:**
  - ACS learns *sample importance* and uses coresets to QAT only the most influential subset.
  - DL-QAT learns *group-specific scales + LoRA adapters* in quantized weight space, updating <1% of parameters.

- **Resilience and robustness:**
  - ACS inherently discounts noisy samples, improving robustness under label noise.
  - DL-QAT re-uses pre-trained structure and only adjusts low-rank directions and scales, preserving most of the original function.

**Relation to val_bpb + INT8+zlib hypothesis**

- For a **parameter-gated lane (gate budget)** and **artifact size limit**, these works suggest:
  - Focus QAT capacity where it matters (high-error samples, sensitive weight groups).
  - Avoid end-to-end retraining of all weights; instead, adjust *quantization scales and low-rank corrections* that can also be tuned to respect **compression sensitivity** (e.g., groups that are hard to compress losslessly with zlib could receive more flexible scales/LoRA).

- ACS-like coreset selection could be adapted to language models by:
  - Defining importance in terms of **val_bpb sensitivity under quantized+compressed weights**, not just loss.
  - Restricting QAT to batches that display large **post-roundtrip loss gaps** vs float.

- DL-QAT suggests a blueprint for **low-overhead staged QAT**:
  - Stage 1: freeze float weights, learn scale parameters and small LoRA modules targeted at INT8 regions that are likely to be perturbed by zlib (e.g., layers whose quantized tensors compress poorly).
  - Stage 2: fix the quantization format and LoRA, maybe lightly finetune for compression artifacts.

**Tensions / diversity**

- ACS is data-centric; DL-QAT is parameter-centric.
- Neither explicitly considers **codec-aware training** (effects of zlib), but both provide mechanisms to restrict training cost within the **Quick-gate** (≤1.10× runtime) envelope.

## Cluster 2 – Low-/Mixed-Precision QAT Algorithms & Scaling Laws

**Papers:** hasan2024optimizing, chen2025scaling, liang2025gplq

**Common ideas**

- **Systematic PTQ vs QAT trade-offs & bit allocation:**
  - hasan2024optimizing analyzes INT8/INT4 PTQ & QAT, size/perf/power trade-offs and proposes theoretical mixed-precision allocations.
  - chen2025scaling derives a **scaling law for QAT** error as a function of model size, data, group size, and shows **activation vs weight error decomposition**.
  - liang2025gplq proposes an **activation-first, weights-later** sequential strategy: brief activation QAT, then PTQ for weights.

- **Practical QAT that is “just enough”:**
  - GPLQ demonstrates that **very short QAT** (1 epoch) focusing on activations can approximate full QAT for ViTs, preserving downstream generalization.

**Relation to val_bpb + INT8+zlib hypothesis**

- These works imply that:
  - Under a strict **runtime gate** and **artifact limit**, **short-stage QAT** (as in GPLQ) is viable: quantize activations or specific int8 groups, align to compression, then freeze.
  - **Mixed-precision** could be adapted to “mixed-compressibility”: layers whose quantized weights compress poorly with zlib could:
    - Receive finer quantization (smaller groups or higher bits), or
    - Remain float or higher-precision while others are INT8.

- The scaling law (chen2025scaling) is crucial for planning:
  - Suggests where QAT effort pays off most, and warns that error depends strongly on **group size** and training tokens.
  - For a **compression-aligned setup**, we can treat compression artifacts as part of the effective “group error,” steering group sizes and bit-widths to balance quantization + compression noise.

**Tensions / diversity**

- GPLQ is developed for ViTs; its two-stage recipe might not transfer 1:1 to LLMs, but the principle “brief QAT to preserve basin + cheap weight quantization” is applicable.
- Scaling-law work is W4A4-focused; our lane is INT8 + zlib. Still, methodology (decompose error sources, focus on bottlenecks) is directly relevant.
