## Cluster 1 – Compression-Efficient Compliance Architectures

**Representative works**  
- PACT (Fang et al. 2025)  
- M-PACE (Verma et al. 2025)  
- AI fraud detection adoption (John et al. 2025)  
- Explainability in RE (Shah et al. 2025)

**Shared idea**  
All four are about structuring complex, heterogeneous compliance workloads so they can be processed efficiently and acceptably under constraints—essentially minimizing “compliance bits per useful decision.”

**Key patterns**

- **Representation compression as a compliance enabler**
  - PACT uses a *single embedding space + graph* to compress millions of artifacts into a structure where retrieval is cheap and high-yield (recall@1 18% → 53%).
  - M-PACE uses a *single multimodal pass* instead of fragmented pipelines; mother–child routing reduces cost by >31× while preserving accuracy.
  - Both are practical analogues of **val_bpb minimization**: maximize compliance-relevant information per unit of model call / storage / compute.

- **Gating under resource ceilings**
  - M-PACE’s child models act as a cheap “first-pass,” with the mother model as a selective, expensive gate—very close to a *quick-harness* that only escalates when needed.
  - PACT serves as a *candidate generator* so that downstream agents avoid brute-force search over all artifacts.
  - Nigerian banks and RE practitioners both describe implicit gates: only some use cases and artifacts can be pushed through AI, constrained by infra, cost, and explainability.

- **Compliance-specific constraints as design drivers**
  - Nigerian banks: high implementation cost is a primary barrier; infra and management support are key positive drivers. This strongly favors *parameter- and cost-efficient* designs.
  - RE: explainability and traceability are non-negotiable; any compression or gating must preserve auditable links between inputs, internal states, and outputs.

**Implications for parameter golf / val_bpb**

- There is strong empirical support for **two-stage or multi-stage architectures**:
  - Stage 1: cheap, compressed representation; approximate decisions.
  - Stage 2: selective, expensive gate for hard or high-risk cases.
- Effective systems minimize:
  - **Bits per useful compliance decision** (calls, parameters, tokens).
  - **Human-review bits** (how often humans must intervene).
- But they must preserve:
  - **Traceability bits** (links needed for audit and certification).
