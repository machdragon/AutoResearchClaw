## Cluster 1 – Staged / Curriculum QAT & Partial Training

**Representative works**
- **CR-QAT (park2026crqat)** – curriculum, module-wise staged quantization for OVOD.
- **GPLQ (liang2025gplq)** – 2-stage ViT QAT: activation QAT first, then weight PTQ.
- **DL-QAT (ke2025dlqat)** – low-rank adapters; <1% params trained under QAT.
- **Adaptive Coreset Selection (huang2023efficient)** – sample-efficient QAT via per-epoch coreset.

**Core ideas**

- **Staged/curriculum quantization**  
  - CR-QAT progressively quantizes submodules, explicitly controlling where quant noise is injected and letting the network adapt gradually.  
  - GPLQ’s “activations-first then weight PTQ” shows that separating activation and weight quantization in time stabilizes optimization and preserves the original basin.

- **Partial-parameter QAT**  
  - DL-QAT uses low-rank adapters and group-wise scales to approximate full-parameter QAT quality with minimal trainable parameters—important for fast, cheap finetuning.

- **Sample-efficient QAT**  
  - ACS (huang) uses disagreement/error metrics to preferentially train on high-signal, low-noise samples each epoch, reducing data and compute requirements.

**Relevance to lane_3**

- Strong support for **staged QAT** as a design principle. For roundtrip-aware compression, you can:
  - Stage 1: introduce **int8 fake-quant & entropy-proxy loss** on activations (and maybe a small subset of weights) while still training mostly in float.  
  - Stage 2: solidify int8 weights + explicit zlib (or differentiable proxy) and run a short, focused QAT stage.

- Partial training (DL-QAT) and coreset QAT (ACS) give knobs to **stay within runtime ≤ 1.10×** by:
  - training only small low-rank adapters +
  - using fewer, more-informative gates/samples per QAT step.

## Cluster 2 – Low-Bit and Exotic-Format QAT for Transformers & LLMs

**Representative works**
- **Attn-QAT (zhang2026attnqat)** – stable FP4 attention via matching forward/backward precision.
- **DL-QAT (ke2025dlqat)** – W3…W4 LLM QAT with decomposed low-rank updates.
- **1-Bit Wonder (maskey2026wonder)** – k-means QAT for extreme low-bit, notably 1-bit weights.
- **Scaling Law for QAT (chen2025scaling)** – W4A4 scaling behavior across model sizes/tokens.
- **Comparative PTQ vs QAT (hasan2024optimizing)** – mixed-precision and γ scaling for INT8/INT4.

**Core ideas**

- **Format-specific stability constraints**  
  - Attn-QAT: forward/backward mismatch in precision causes major instability; using identical low precision for attention in both directions re-stabilizes QAT. Analog: for int8+zlib, you may need **consistent quantization and symbolization** in both training and evaluation paths.

- **Non-uniform / k-means quantization**  
  - 1-Bit Wonder: k-means codebooks significantly outperform uniform integer quantization at same bit budget; in 1-bit regime this is decisive.

- **Low-bit scaling behavior**  
  - Scaling-law work: activation outliers (especially in FC2) dominate W4A4 error; mixed-precision or layer-wise targeting is needed.  
  - Increasing training tokens doesn’t monotonically fix quantization error; weight error can dominate given enough tokens.

- **Mixed-precision benefits**  
  - Hasan’s study: per-layer sensitivity and variance can guide bit allocation, significantly improving efficiency/perf trade-offs.

**Relevance to lane_3**

- Even though our target is **int8**, these works suggest:
  - You should **treat the entropy-coding pipeline like a “format” with its own stability quirks**—e.g., rare symbol explosions or heavy tails in residuals.
  - Layer-wise **mixed precision** (e.g., keep extremely outlier-heavy layers at higher precision) may greatly reduce entropy while staying within compute gates.

- K-means ideas may translate to:
  - **non-uniform int8 codebooks** (e.g., clustering per-tensor or per-channel prior to entropy coding), aligning quantized distributions with zlib-friendly symbol statistics.
