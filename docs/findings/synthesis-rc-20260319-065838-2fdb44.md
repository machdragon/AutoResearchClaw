## Cluster 1 – Safety Boundary Modeling & Artifact-Constrained Optimization

**Included:** Heverin (2026), Weber et al. (2024)

### Shared Theme

Both works address **how models behave as functions of data and prompts under constraints**, and how to structure evaluation/training artifacts and metrics:

- **Heverin (2026)** treats refusal as a **local decision boundary** in prompt space, showing:
  - High average refusal rates (>94%) but **substantial local instability**.
  - Strong **artifact dependence**: textual ransomware artifacts are much more prone to “refusal escapes” than executable malware artifacts.
  - Introduction of **Refusal Boundary Entropy (RBE)** to quantify instability across structured perturbations.
- **Weber et al. (2024)** provide:
  - An open, large-scale corpus (RedPajama V1/V2) with **quality signals and metadata**.
  - Empirical evidence that **quality-aware filtering** improves downstream performance.
  - A transparent foundation for **reproducible model development** and curation policies.

### Synthesis for “val_bpb minimization under quick-harness gate, with 16MB artifact compliance”

1. **Objective vs. Constraints**
   - Val_bpb minimization (analogous to perplexity minimization) must be framed **jointly** with:
     - **Safety constraints**: refusal behavior must be robust, not just average-case safe.
     - **Artifact constraints**: certain artifact types (e.g., executable code, large binaries, 16MB size cap) may require stricter policies or different handling.
   - Heverin implies that **evaluating only single prompts** (or only aggregate refusal rates) is misleading; one must consider **local neighborhoods** in prompt space.

2. **Quick-Harness Evaluation**
   - A “quick-harness gate” suggests a **fast, automated promotion test** (e.g., a limited set of prompts + artifacts).
   - Heverin’s results show this is **insufficient** if:
     - It does not explore structured perturbation families.
     - It does not stratify by artifact type.
   - For a promotion harness, we need:
     - **Local robustness metrics** (e.g., RBE or similar) per base prompt.
     - Coverage across **artifact classes** with known differential risk.

3. **Artifact and Metadata Use**
   - Weber’s dataset design shows how **metadata and quality signals** can be leveraged to:
     - Enforce **artifact-type compliance** (e.g., filter or tag artifacts >16MB, code vs text, etc.).
     - Provide **structured evaluation slices** (by domain, length, source).
   - For a 16MB artifact cap, training and evaluation corpora should:
     - Include **explicit artifact-size metadata**.
     - Allow **size-aware filtering** and **size-stratified evaluation**.

4. **Metrics Beyond val_bpb**
   - Heverin introduces **RBE**; Weber uses standard perplexity + downstream metrics.
   - A realistic gate must jointly track:
     - **Core objective**: val_bpb / perplexity.
     - **Safety robustness**: refusal rate, flip rate, RBE-like instability metrics.
     - **Compliance metrics**: fraction of outputs respecting artifact size/type rules.
