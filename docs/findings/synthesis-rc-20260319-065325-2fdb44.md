## Cluster 1 – Compression & Parameter Golf Under Operational Gates

**Core theme:**  
Aggressively minimizing resource usage or “parameter budget” (val_bpb, model size, inference cost) under hard gates (e.g., 16MB artifact, safety refusal thresholds, latency/cost budgets) while maintaining compliance and task performance.

**Key contributions:**

- **M-PACE (verma2025mpace)**  
  - Mother–child MLLM architecture that dynamically routes multimodal compliance checks to cheaper child models.  
  - Achieves >31× cost reduction ($0.0005 vs. $0.0159 per image) at similar accuracy by intelligently allocating capacity.  
  - Embodies “parameter golf” at *runtime*: maximize compliance classification under a cost gate.

- **Refusal Boundary Instability (heverin2026prompt)**  
  - Shows that safety refusal is *locally unstable*: ~⅓ of base prompts have at least one perturbation that flips refusal to (partial/full) compliance.  
  - Artifact type strongly conditions robustness: textual threats much less stable than executable malware.  
  - For “parameter golf” on safety, this implies that optimizing a single refusal metric at one prompt is insufficient; one must minimize *entropy* of the refusal boundary under structured perturbations.

- **Artifacts-as-Signal EEG (eldin2025artifacts)**  
  - Not about compression per se, but about *information efficiency*: a single Kuramoto phase metric R captures hidden structure missed by voltage-only views.  
  - Shows that discarding “artifacts” throws away ~70% of cognitively relevant signal; a better representation (phase synchronization) yields a more compressed, causally structured view.  
  - For val_bpb minimization, this is analogous to designing representations that preserve causal structure under a tight bit budget.

**Synthesis to topic:**  
- Parameter golf under a **quick-harness gate** (fast evaluation harness, safety filters) is not just about lower val_bpb; it is about **where** and **how** capacity is spent:
  - Use *hierarchical routing* (M-PACE) to keep most queries on low-parameter paths and only escalate when needed.
  - Use *information-efficient representations* (phase synchronization-style metrics) so that each stored/served artifact (≤16MB) carries maximally structured signal.
  - Evaluate safety/compliance not only at a single prompt but under **perturbation families**, minimizing refusal-boundary entropy as a constraint alongside val_bpb.

**Core theme:**  
Building structured, embedding-based graphs to connect heterogeneous artifacts (code, docs, tools, models, logs) so that compliance and safety systems can discover the *right* small artifact under size and cost constraints.

- **PACT (fang2025privacy)**  
  - Large-scale embedding-driven artifact graph (code, data, tools, docs) for privacy/compliance.  
  - Contrastive fine-tuning boosts recall@1 from 18% → 53%; AI-agent query match from 9.6% → 69.7%.  
  - Demonstrates that *embedding quality* and *graph structure* are critical to discovering relevant compliance artifacts.

  - Though primarily a modeling paper, it implicitly depends on structured representations of multimodal ad artifacts and their compliance attributes.  
  - Acts as a runtime consumer of an underlying “artifact graph” of policies, categories, and prior decisions.
