# Stage 07: Synthesis

## Cluster Overview

1. **Cluster 1 – Parameter Golf under Hard Resource Gates (LLM data & training recipes)**  
   - Focus: How to tune data and training “knobs” (quality filters, corpus composition) to minimize a cost-like objective (analogous to `val_bpb`) under external constraints (here, openness, transparency, and implied compute/storage limits).

2. **Cluster 2 – Process Parameter Optimization under Physical/Material Constraints**  
   - Focus: Classical “parameter golf” in physical processes: rheology and coagulation for polymer spinning, surface texturing via shot peening, and TCO modeling. Each optimizes performance while respecting stringent process or economic constraints.

3. **Cluster 3 – Quality/Defect Modeling and Classification for Fast Feedback Loops**  
   - Focus: Systematic defect/quality taxonomies (FFF printing, EEG under exercise) that could serve as feedback/targets for automated parameter search under quick-harness-like evaluation.

Across clusters, the unifying theme is **structured parameter spaces + constrained evaluation budgets**: how to choose minimal, high-signal measurements or artifacts to drive optimization.

## Cluster 1 – Parameter Golf for LLMs with Data Quality Signals

**Core papers:**  
- Weber et al. 2024 (RedPajama V1/V2)

**Synthesis**

- RedPajama provides:
  - Huge parameterizable data space (web documents + rich quality metadata).
  - A set of **quality signals** that can be treated as tunable “gates” on the training corpus.
- The authors effectively play **data-parameter golf**:
  - Define a target objective (LM performance on benchmarks; conceptually akin to minimizing `val_bpb`).
  - Vary **filter thresholds and mixing strategies** over quality signals.
  - Show that **smaller, higher-quality subsets** can outperform larger, unfiltered data.
- V1 (LLaMA-style mix) and V2 (web-only) provide **transparent recipes** and artifacts, enabling:
  - Reproducible ablations.
  - Fast iteration on filter configurations without regenerating the entire corpus.

**Relation to “quick-harness gate” & 16MB artifact constraint**

- Quick-harness analogy:
  - Train or fine-tune small (≤1.6B) models as **fast evaluation harnesses** to estimate the utility of a given data slice or filter configuration.
  - Use benchmark scores or validation bits-per-byte (or equivalent) as the gate metric.
- 16MB artifact compliance:
  - Implies that **evaluation artifacts** (e.g., logs, metrics, small held-out samples, compact configs) must be compact.
  - RedPajama’s **metadata and quality signals** are inherently low-volume relative to raw text, making them suitable for compact artifacts (e.g., a few kB of stats per experiment).

**Implications**

- RedPajama is a ready-made environment for:
  - Defining a **parameter search space** over quality filters.
  - Using **small models and small evaluation sets** as quick-harnesses.
  - Emitting only minimal artifacts (config + summary metrics) per trial.

## Cluster 2 – Process Parameter Optimization under Physical/Material Constraints

**Core papers:**  
- Skvortsov et al. 2020 (PAPNI rheology/coagulation)  
- Heydari Astaraee et al. 2023 (custom shots for surface texturing)  
- Woody et al. 2024 (TCO of vehicles)

**Synthesis**

1. **Polymer rheology & coagulation (Skvortsov et al.)**
   - Parameter space: temperature, time, concentration, molecular weight, non-solvent content.
   - Objective: maintain solution stability and desired rheology to enable defect-minimized fiber/film formation.
   - Constraint: **temperature–time stability window**; beyond it, irreversible changes occur.
   - Insight: carefully **modulating non-solvent content** accelerates coagulation and reduces defects.

   Analogy:  
   - Temperature–time window ≈ **training stability region**; going outside leads to degradation (overfitting, catastrophic forgetting).
   - Non-solvent dosing ≈ **small, targeted data injections or curriculum steps** that change dynamics without destabilizing.

2. **Shot peening with custom AM shots (Heydari Astaraee et al.)**
   - Parameter space: shot geometry, impact conditions, material.
   - Objective: achieve **specific, controllable surface textures**.
   - Constraint: process must remain compatible with conventional shot peening infrastructure.
   - Insight: **geometry is a high-leverage parameter**; small changes drive qualitatively different texture fields.

   Analogy:  
   - Shot geometry ≈ **data selection/weighting configuration**; small changes in filter thresholds can lead to qualitatively different learned behaviors.
   - FE simulations ≈ **cheap proxy models** to pre-screen configurations before expensive physical trials → akin to quick-harness models before full-scale training.

3. **Vehicle TCO modeling (Woody et al.)**
   - Parameter space: city, powertrain, vehicle class, range, charging access, tariffs.
   - Objective: minimize **lifetime TCO**.
   - Constraints: physical product options, local prices, incentives.
