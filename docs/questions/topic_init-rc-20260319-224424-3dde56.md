# Stage 01: Topic Init

- **Topic**: The broad area  
  Parameter-efficient optimization of neural language models under an explicit Minimum Description Length (MDL) / compression lens, targeting validation bits-per-byte (val_bpb) minimization at a strict deployable artifact size limit (≤16MB) via quantization-aware, entropy-penalized training.

---

### Novel Angle

Most work on compression-aware training and post‑training quantization either:

1. **Optimizes downstream task loss** (e.g., cross‑entropy on tokens) and treats compression (quantization, pruning, coding) as a post‑hoc step, or  
2. **Optimizes an explicit rate–distortion tradeoff** but at the *activation* or *feature* level (e.g., in learned image compression / VAEs), or  
3. **Targets on-device efficiency (latency/energy)** more than strict *final model artifact size* at a fixed performance floor.

Your proposed direction instead focuses on:

> **Artifact-centric objective optimization for language models:** include a *roundtrip proxy loss* (fake int8 quantization + entropy penalty on parameter distributions) directly in the training objective, and evaluate success in terms of *post-roundtrip* validation bits-per-byte (val_bpb) under a hard artifact-size budget (16MB) and a runtime quick-gate (≤1.10× baseline).

This angle is **not yet well-covered** for several reasons:

1. **Objective directly tied to deployable artifact size.**  
   - Existing quantization-aware training (QAT) usually approximates post-training quantization to preserve accuracy, but the objective is still model-side loss (cross-entropy) rather than a *surrogate for compression rate* (e.g., entropy of weight distributions under a concrete coding scheme).  
   - Here, the objective explicitly incorporates:  
     - Simulated int8 quantization *during training* (fake quant), and  
     - An entropy penalty that approximates the actual bits required to store the weights (and possibly codebooks).  
   - The goal is not just “good perplexity under quantization”, but *minimum val_bpb for a given final artifact size*.

2. **MDL framing at parameter level with an *in-training roundtrip constraint*.**  
   - MDL has been applied to neural networks conceptually (e.g., PAC-Bayes & flat minima, implicit regularization by SGD), but rarely as a *practical engineering objective* tied to a specific deployment constraint like a 16MB artifact.  
   - Prior work on “bit allocation” and “entropy-constrained optimization” focuses on activations or intermediate representations (e.g., learned compression for images/audio).  
   - Here, the MDL view is *parameter-centric*: penalize model parameter entropy such that the final, coded model file is small, and test whether that yields better *post-roundtrip val_bpb* at fixed size vs. standard regularization or naive QAT.

3. **Tight, realistic constraints: fixed 16MB artifact, quick-gate on training/runtime, and val_bpb (not perplexity) as the main metric.**  
   - Most LLM compression papers focus on relative perplexity or task accuracy under compression, often with large models and flexible size budgets.  
   - A strict 16MB limit forces non-trivial tradeoffs that standard QAT/pruning heuristics might not optimally navigate.  
   - Using val_bpb (bits per byte of text) as the primary metric ties the model’s quality directly to practical compression performance (as in GPT-2 / GPT-Neo style text modeling), which is only partially correlated with token-level perplexity due to tokenization and dataset specifics.

4. **Timeliness (2024–2026).**  
   Recent developments make this particularly opportune:
   - Edge and on-device LLM deployment is accelerating, with model *artifact size* and *update bandwidth* becoming a practical bottleneck.  
   - There is active interest in MDL and information-theoretic views of NN generalization and compression, but most deployed methods do not optimize a concrete “bits on disk” objective.  
   - Tooling for fake quant, per-channel quantization, and learned entropy models is now mature enough to simulate realistic roundtrips with low overhead—making this kind of training feasible on a single GPU.

**How this differs from standard approaches**

- **Not** just QAT: You are adding a *parameter entropy penalty* calibrated to an actual coding scheme—effectively an *in-training learned codec* for weight tensors, evaluated through val_bpb of the model on text.  
- **Not** just pruning + quantization: You are explicitly testing whether incorporating the roundtrip and entropy into the loss **improves post-roundtrip val_bpb at a fixed 16MB cap**, versus training a model and then compressing it afterwards.  
- **Not** just MDL as a theoretical lens: You use MDL as a concrete engineering constraint on disk size, combined with a runtime quick‑gate (≤1.10× baseline).
