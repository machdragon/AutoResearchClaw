# Stage 08: Hypothesis Gen

Here is a synthesized set of final hypotheses that keeps the strongest ideas, addresses contrarian critiques, and stays within the pragmatist’s feasibility constraints. I’ll give 3 hypotheses, each with: rationale, measurable prediction, and failure condition, and I’ll flag unresolved disagreements explicitly.

## Hypothesis 1 – Entropy‑shaped INT8 formats beat codec‑in‑the‑loop QAT

**Claim (format-first vs codec-in-loop):**  
Under a fixed architecture, training budget, gate budget, and ≤16MB INT8+zlib artifact cap, **carefully designed, entropy‑shaped INT8 formats plus standard/lightweight QAT or tuned PTQ** will match or outperform **codec‑in‑the‑loop staged QAT** on post‑roundtrip val_bpb.

In other words: shaping the **INT8 representation and layout** (codebooks, clustering, layout) is the main lever; putting zlib into the training loop gives, at most, marginal extra gains.

### Rationale

- From the innovator:
  - Entropy-shaping of int8 weights (clustered values, repeated patterns, low empirical entropy) taps directly into what zlib exploits.
  - There is clear conceptual leverage in training toward low-entropy int8 distributions (e.g., few codebook entries) rather than ignoring the codec.

- From the contrarian:
  - zlib is lossless and oblivious to semantics. Once the INT8 representation and layout are fixed, zlib’s roundtrip is the identity on values; you only see quantization noise, not “codec noise.”
  - The codec operates on global byte statistics; that is better addressed via representation design and entropy control than via rare, noisy gradients through zlib.
  - Training-time budget (≤1.10×) makes frequent codec-in-the-loop calls hard to justify.

- From the pragmatist:
  - QAT is already effective at adapting to quantization noise with minimal training.
  - Extra codec calls must be sparse; if the main effect can be achieved by shaping the quantizer / layout (plus maybe entropy regularization), that is simpler and cheaper.

**Unresolved disagreement:**  
The innovator believes that *directly* penalizing empirical entropy or compressibility during QAT (entropy-shaped QAT) adds extra benefit beyond just choosing a better format; the contrarian argues that once formats are zlib‑friendly, codec-aligned QAT yields negligible additional gains.

This hypothesis essentially sides with the contrarian on *relative importance*: format/entropy > codec-in-loop, but still allows entropy regularization as part of QAT/PTQ.

### Concrete experimental comparison

Compare two methods on the same small LM (e.g., 20–50M params), same training tokens, gate/compute budget, and ≤16MB artifact limit.

- **Method A – Codec-aligned staged QAT (baseline from pragmatist/innovator):**
  - Standard per-channel INT8 format, conventional layout.
  - Staged QAT with occasional true INT8→zlib→decompress roundtrip on weights in the forward path (as in pragmatist Hypothesis 1).
  - No special entropy shaping or codebook constraints.

- **Method B – Entropy-shaped format + standard QAT/PTQ (ours):**
  - No zlib in the training loop.
  - Design INT8 representation and serialization to be zlib-friendly:
    - E.g., constrain each large weight tensor to a small codebook (say 16–32 distinct INT8 values) via codebook-based quantization or clustering; optionally share codebooks across layers.
    - Arrange layout (e.g., block order, grouping) to maximize repeated local patterns (whole repeated kernels, block sparsity, shared tiles).
  - Use:
    - Either tuned PTQ into this constrained format plus a short float finetune,
    - Or short QAT with fake quantization into this codebook/format (no codec calls).
  - Optionally include a cheap entropy proxy regularizer on the quantized weights:
    - `L_total = L_ce + λ * H(quanted_weights)` or a clipped version `max(0, H - H_target)`.

**Primary metric**: post‑roundtrip val_bpb after exporting int8 weights in each method’s format, applying zlib, then decompressing and running int8 inference.

### Measurable prediction

Let:

- `bpb_codecQAT`: best val_bpb from Method A (codec-in-loop staged QAT).
- `bpb_entropyFormat`: best val_bpb from Method B (entropy-shaped format + standard QAT/PTQ).

**Prediction 1.1:**

- `bpb_entropyFormat ≤ bpb_codecQAT`  
  i.e., well-designed entropy-shaped INT8 formats plus standard QAT/PTQ **match or beat** codec-aligned QAT with naive formats.
- Additionally, Method B will achieve:
  - Equal or better zlib compression ratio (smaller compressed size for same architecture), or
  - Equal artifact size with better accuracy.

### Failure condition

Hypothesis 1 is rejected if, after reasonable tuning of codebooks and layouts:

- `bpb_entropyFormat > 1.01 * bpb_codecQAT`  
  (more than 1% relative *worse*), **and** this gap is consistent across:
  - Multiple seeds, and
  - At least two datasets / domain variants,

under matched training steps, runtime gate (≤1.10×), and ≤16MB artifacts.
