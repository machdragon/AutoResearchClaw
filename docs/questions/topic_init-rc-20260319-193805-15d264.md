# Stage 01: Topic Init

- **Topic**:  
  Parameter-efficient training and compression-aware optimization for val_bpb minimization under tight deployment and artifact constraints.

---

### Novel Angle

Most work on quantization-aware training (QAT) and model compression focuses on preserving *task metrics* (e.g., accuracy, perplexity) under low-precision deployment. Separately, compression-aware optimization for language models (e.g., bits-per-byte / bits-per-character) typically assumes either (a) floating-point training with post-hoc compression or (b) quantization that is *not* explicitly aligned to the real deployment roundtrip (quantize → compress → dequantize / decode) and storage constraints.

The **novel angle** here is:

> **Staged QAT explicitly aligned to an int8+zlib roundtrip, optimized for post-roundtrip validation bits-per-byte (val_bpb) under a *fixed gate budget* and *hard artifact size limit (≤16MB)*, with a runtime “quick-gate” (≤1.10× baseline).**

Concretely, this differs from standard approaches in several ways:

1. **Objective alignment to real deployment pipeline**  
   - Instead of minimizing val_bpb in float32 and *assuming* that int8+zlib storage and decoding fidelity follow, we explicitly:
     - Train/stage QAT such that model parameters are *jointly optimized* for:
       1. int8 quantization effects, and  
       2. downstream zlib compression behavior on the serialized weights.  
     - Evaluate **val_bpb after a full roundtrip**: float → int8 → serialized (zlib) → deserialized → int8 inference.
   - This is stricter than typical “quantization-aware” objectives, which usually stop at matching float vs quantized predictions, without modeling the subsequent entropy coder.

2. **Gate-budget–aware compression alignment**  
   - “Gate budget” here refers to a fixed training/control budget (e.g., number of compression/quantization “gates” or total training steps allocated to compression-aware updates vs baseline updates, similar in spirit to parameter-efficient “gating” of which parameters may change).
   - Prior work on compression-aware training tends not to explicitly couple **optimization schedule** (staged QAT) with:
     - an explicit *gate budget* constraint, and  
     - a hard **artifacts-size constraint** (≤16MB repository artifact, e.g., for open-source release or system deployment).
   - We study how to *stage* QAT (e.g., late-phase QAT, progressive per-layer activation) to respect:
     - compute and runtime bounds (≤1.10× baseline), and  
     - artifact size bounds,  
     while still improving **post-roundtrip val_bpb** over a float-only baseline.

3. **Target metric: val_bpb under compression-aware quantization, not just accuracy or perplexity**  
   - Recent work on LLM compression largely optimizes perplexity or downstream accuracy and only *reports* compression metrics secondarily.
   - Here, **val_bpb after int8+zlib roundtrip** is the primary metric, positioning this as a compression-optimized modeling problem rather than just a quantization robustness study.

4. **Timeliness (2024–2026)**  
   Recent developments make this specifically timely:
   - **LZ + transformer compression trend**: There is growing interest in using transformers for text/code compression and as drop-in replacements or boosters for classic compressors.
   - **Edge/embedded deployment of small LMs**: Many practical deployments now care about *artifact size*, *runtime*, and *simple codecs* (e.g., zlib) more than about squeezing the last 0.1 perplexity point.
   - **Parameter-efficient, budgeted training**: Parameter-golf–style schemes and “small artifact” competitions push for *fully reproducible*, highly compressed models with strict constraints on size and training time.

To the best of current knowledge, there is **no systematic study** that:

- Fixes a **gate/training budget and runtime quick-gate**,  
- Enforces a **16MB artifact limit**,  
- And compares **float-only vs staged QAT explicitly aligned to the int8+zlib roundtrip**  
with **val_bpb after roundtrip** as the primary optimization target.

Existing QAT and compression-aware works either:
- ignore an explicit downstream general-purpose compressor like zlib,  
- do not enforce tight artifact limits,  
- or optimize classical task metrics rather than end-to-end val_bpb.
