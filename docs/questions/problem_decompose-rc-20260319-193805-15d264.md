# Stage 02: Problem Decompose

## Source

Research topic: **Parameter Golf val_bpb minimization — lane_3: QAT + roundtrip-aware compression alignment**

Core hypothesis:  
Enabling **staged QAT explicitly aligned to an int8+zlib roundtrip** will **improve post-roundtrip validation bits-per-byte (val_bpb)** versus **float-only training** under:

- the **same gate/training budget**,  
- a **16MB compressed artifact limit**, and  
- a **runtime quick-gate** of `runtime_factor ≤ 1.10` relative to baseline.

---

## Sub-questions

1. **Effectiveness: Does roundtrip-aligned QAT actually improve val_bpb under constraints?**  
   - 1.1: For a fixed small transformer architecture that meets the ≤16MB int8+zlib artifact limit, what is the **delta in `val_bpb_roundtrip`** between:
     - (A) float-only training with post-hoc int8+zlib, and  
     - (B) staged QAT explicitly aligned to the int8+zlib roundtrip,  
     **under the same gate budget and training time**?  
   - 1.2: Can we reliably achieve the target **≥0.03 bpb reduction** in `val_bpb_roundtrip` with QAT+roundtrip across multiple seeds or runs?  
   - 1.3: How sensitive is the val_bpb gain to model size and capacity near the 16MB artifact boundary (e.g., slightly under- vs fully-packing the size budget)?

2. **Staging & gate-budget design: What QAT schedule works best under quick-gate and gate constraints?**  
   - 2.1: Among several **staging strategies** (e.g., late-phase QAT only, gradual per-layer QAT, alternating QAT and float phases), which yields the best **val_bpb_roundtrip vs runtime_factor trade-off**?  
   - 2.2: How should the **gate budget** (fraction of steps or updates devoted to QAT/roundtrip-aware objectives vs baseline training) be allocated to maximize `val_bpb_roundtrip` improvement while keeping `runtime_factor ≤ 1.10`?  
   - 2.3: Is there a **minimal QAT duration or intensity** (fraction of training) beyond which additional QAT brings diminishing returns to val_bpb, given the same total training steps?

3. **Zlib alignment: How to incorporate zlib behavior into training in a practical, low-overhead way?**  
   - 3.1: Which **zlib-aware training signal** is most effective under the runtime constraint:  
     - direct periodic zlib compression of weights with feedback,  
     - differentiable or proxy regularizers that promote zlib-friendly weight statistics (e.g., sparsity, low entropy of deltas), or  
     - structural constraints (e.g., weight sharing, low-rank patterns) that indirectly help zlib?  
   - 3.2: How frequently can we **invoke real zlib roundtrip** during training (or at checkpoints) without violating `runtime_factor ≤ 1.10`, and does more frequent feedback materially improve `val_bpb_roundtrip`?  
   - 3.3: Do different zlib-friendly regularization strategies **change the empirical weight distribution and compressed artifact size** in interpretable ways (e.g., histogram concentration, run-length patterns) that correlate with val_bpb gains?

4. **Constraint satisfaction & trade-offs: How do artifact size and runtime constraints interact with val_bpb?**  
   - 4.1: For both baseline and QAT+roundtrip pipelines, what is the **Pareto frontier** over `val_bpb_roundtrip`, `artifact_size_bytes`, and `runtime_factor`, and where does the best feasible configuration sit under ≤16MB and ≤1.10× runtime?  
   - 4.2: Does pushing the artifact **closer to 16MB** (e.g., larger model or less aggressive pruning) systematically improve `val_bpb_roundtrip`, or do zlib/quantization interactions produce non-monotonic behavior?  
   - 4.3: Are there **failure modes** where meeting the runtime quick-gate forces weaker QAT or zlib feedback, negating any val_bpb benefit versus float-only training?

5. **Robustness and generality: Are the observed gains stable and not benchmark-specific?**  
   - 5.1: Are the improvements in `val_bpb_roundtrip` from staged roundtrip-aware QAT **consistent across random seeds** and modest variations in data split or tokenization?  
   - 5.2: When transferring the best-found strategy to a **slightly different text compression setting** (e.g., smaller corpus or different vocabulary), do we still see meaningful `val_bpb_roundtrip` gains under the same artifact/runtime constraints?  
   - 5.3: How robust is the method to **implementation choices** (PyTorch vs JAX QAT primitives, per-tensor vs per-channel int8, different calibration schemes) regarding both val_bpb and artifact size?
