# Stage 02: Problem Decompose

## Source

Topic and hypothesis (user-provided):

> Parameter Golf val_bpb minimization — lane_3: QAT + roundtrip-aware compression alignment.  
> Hypothesis: If we enable staged QAT aligned to int8+zlib roundtrip, post-roundtrip val_bpb will improve versus float-only training under the same gate budget (runtime ≤ 1.10× baseline, artifact ≤ 16 MB).

Goal: Decompose this into prioritized research sub-questions.

---

## Sub-questions

### Q1. Does staged, roundtrip-aware QAT actually improve *post-roundtrip* val_bpb under fixed gate budget and artifact constraints?

- **What to ask**
  - Compared to a strong float-only baseline trained under the same data/architecture and gate budget, does staged QAT that is explicitly aligned to an int8→zlib→int8 roundtrip:
    - reduce **post-roundtrip val_bpb** on validation text?
    - do so while:
      - keeping **training runtime ≤ 1.10×** the float baseline, and
      - satisfying the **16 MB artifact limit** after int8 packing + zlib?
- **What needs to be specified**
  - Clear baselines:
    - Float-only training → post-hoc int8 quantization → zlib (no QAT).
    - Optional: vanilla QAT (no zlib-awareness) → post-hoc zlib.
  - One or two concrete staged-QAT schedules that include simulated roundtrips.
  - Common evaluation protocol for val_bpb after a real int8+zlib roundtrip.

---

### Q2. Which **staging strategies** and **zlib-aware mechanisms** in QAT matter most for val_bpb and compressibility?

- **What to ask**
  - How do different QAT curricula compare, under the same runtime and artifact limits?
    - Stage onset: early vs mid vs late introduction of fake-quant.
    - Transition schedule: abrupt vs gradual increase of quantization “strength.”
    - Frequency and granularity of simulated roundtrips (per step, per N steps, per layer subset, per checkpoint).
  - Which explicit **zlib-aligned mechanisms** help:
    - regularizers targeting weight entropy / pattern repetition,
    - per-tensor vs per-channel scaling tuned for compressibility,
    - layout / packing choices that enhance DEFLATE compressibility (e.g., ordering of tensors).
- **What needs to be measured**
  - For each schedule/mechanism:
    - post-roundtrip val_bpb,
    - compressed artifact size and compression ratio,
    - runtime overhead attributable to staged QAT and zlib simulations.

---

### Q3. How do quantization and zlib roundtrip affect **weight statistics, model behavior, and the trade-off between pre-quantization loss and post-roundtrip val_bpb?**

- **What to ask**
  - How do weight distributions evolve under:
    - float-only training,
    - vanilla QAT,
    - roundtrip-aware QAT?
  - Are there observable changes that correlate with better **zlib compressibility** and **lower val_bpb**, such as:
    - lower empirical entropy of weights,
    - more repeated patterns/blocks,
    - clustering of values around a small set of int8 codes?
  - What is the relationship between:
    - pre-quantization validation loss (or token-level perplexity),
    - post-quantization (int8 only) performance,
    - post-roundtrip (int8+zlib) val_bpb?
  - Does optimizing for post-roundtrip val_bpb incur a meaningful cost in standard validation loss, and is that cost acceptable under the success criteria?

---

### Q4. How tight are the **runtime (≤1.10×)** and **16 MB artifact** constraints in practice, and what levers exist to stay within them while still gaining from QAT?

- **What to ask**
  - Where does overhead actually come from?
    - fake-quant ops in forward/backward,
    - extra copies / shadow parameters,
    - serialized roundtrip checks (int8 pack + zlib compress/decompress),
    - logging / measurement infrastructure.
  - Under which configurations do we violate the 1.10× quick-gate, and how can we:
    - reduce roundtrip frequency,
    - subsample tensors or layers for roundtrip simulation,
    - adjust model size or sequence length,
    - or simplify the QAT schedule,
    without losing the main val_bpb gains?
  - How close does the best-performing configuration come to the 16 MB limit?
    - Is there headroom or do we need additional constraints (e.g., pruning or smaller architectures) to fit under 16 MB once all necessary metadata is included?

---
