# Stage 02: Problem Decompose

## Source

Research problem (paraphrased):  
Investigate whether **staged quantization-aware training (QAT)**, explicitly aligned with an **int8 + zlib roundtrip pipeline**, can **improve post-roundtrip validation bits-per-byte (val_bpb)** compared to a float-only baseline, **under the same gate budget**, with a **model artifact ≤16MB** and **training runtime overhead ≤1.10×**.

---

## Sub-questions

### Q1. Baseline + feasibility: What is the achievable float and naive-int8+zlib baseline frontier under the 16MB and 1.10× constraints?

**Intent**: Establish reference points and confirm that the constraints are even satisfiable.

Key aspects:
- Q1.1: For 1–2 candidate Transformer architectures on enwik8, what are:
  - Float-only val_bpb, training runtime, and (hypothetical) serialized size (unconstrained)?
  - Int8 post-hoc quantized val_bpb (no QAT), with and without zlib, and resulting artifact sizes?
- Q1.2: Under a hard 16MB artifact cap, what is the **best float-equivalent configuration** we can deploy (e.g., pruned + int8, but trained as float), and what is its **val_bpb_roundtrip**?
- Q1.3: What is the **runtime cost** of:
  - Periodically running the full `float → int8 → zlib → decompress → int8` loop?
  - Logging val_bpb_roundtrip checkpoints?
  - How frequently can we afford to run roundtrips while staying within **≤1.10×** total training time?
- Q1.4: How sensitive is zlib-compressed size to simple factors (e.g., tensor ordering, grouping, metadata headers) at fixed int8 weights?

---

### Q2. QAT design: Which staged QAT schedules and quantization schemes give the best tradeoff between accuracy, compressibility, and runtime?

**Intent**: Define the actual QAT knobs and schedules that might yield gains.

Key aspects:
- Q2.1: Among candidate QAT strategies, which are most promising under the runtime cap?
  - Late QAT (enable int8 emulation only in the final X% of steps).
  - Gradual layer-wise QAT (progressively converting layers to int8).
  - Gradual precision schedule (e.g., int16 → int8).
- Q2.2: What granularity and scheme for int8 quantization are compatible with both:
  - Good LM accuracy (val_bpb),
  - Good zlib compressibility (e.g., per-tensor vs per-channel, symmetric vs asymmetric, shared scales)?
- Q2.3: How does the **frequency and duration** of QAT phases impact:
  - Final val_bpb (pre- and post-roundtrip),
  - Training stability,
  - Runtime overhead?
- Q2.4: What minimal additional QAT-specific operations (fake quantization ops, observers, calibration passes) can be used so that the overall training runtime remains ≤1.10×?

---

### Q3. Roundtrip-aware alignment: Which training-time proxies and heuristics correlate with better int8+zlib roundtrip behavior, and how to integrate them without breaking the runtime budget?

**Intent**: Make training *aware* of the non-differentiable codec despite not backpropagating through zlib.

Key aspects:
- Q3.1: Which **differentiable proxies** for zlib behavior correlate best with:
  - Lower zlib-compressed size at fixed int8 format,
  - Better val_bpb_roundtrip?
  Candidates: histogram sparsity/peakedness of weights, L1/L2 regularization patterns, clustering toward a few discrete values, per-block variance penalties, structural constraints (e.g., tying or block replication).
- Q3.2: Can we design **block-wise or layout constraints** (e.g., group weights by similar statistics, reorder tensors) that:
  - Improve zlib compressibility,
  - Are stable under QAT,
  - Do not require major runtime overhead?
- Q3.3: How often should we run **full roundtrip evaluations** during training (e.g., every N steps, only during late QAT) to:
  - Provide a useful signal for model/HP selection,
  - Stay within the runtime quick-gate?
- Q3.4: Does adding these proxies/heuristics during QAT:
  - Improve Δval_bpb_roundtrip vs. float-only by ≥0.02,
  - Without violating the 16MB and 1.10× constraints?

---

### Q4. Comparative evaluation: Under a fixed gate budget, does staged, roundtrip-aware QAT *actually* improve post-roundtrip val_bpb versus a matched float-only baseline?

**Intent**: Directly test the central hypothesis with controlled experiments.

Key aspects:
- Q4.1: For the **best-performing QAT+roundtrip configuration(s)** from Q2–Q3:
  - What are val_bpb_roundtrip, artifact size, and train_runtime_ratio?
- Q4.2: When matched on:
  - Architecture,
  - Token budget,
  - Wall-clock budget (≤1.10×),
  how does QAT+roundtrip compare to:
  - Float-only training with post-hoc int8+zlib export,
  - Float-only training with any parameter/pruning tweaks needed to hit 16MB?
- Q4.3: Is the target **Δval_bpb ≤ -0.02** consistently achieved across:
  - Different random seeds,
  - At least two QAT staging variants (e.g., late vs gradual)?
- Q4.4: What is the **tradeoff frontier** between:
  - Artifact size,
  - val_bpb_roundtrip,
  - Runtime,
  and where does the proposed method sit relative to naive baselines?
