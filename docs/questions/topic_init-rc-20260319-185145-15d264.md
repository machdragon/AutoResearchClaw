# Stage 01: Topic Init

- **Topic**: Parameter Golf val_bpb minimization — lane_3: QAT + roundtrip-aware compression alignment

---

## Novel Angle

Most work on quantization-aware training (QAT) and model compression optimizes either:

1. **Inference-time metrics** (accuracy, perplexity, bpb) *before* external compression, or  
2. **Compression ratios** using post‑hoc coding (e.g., ZIP/zlib, entropy coding) applied to fixed-precision weights.

What has **not** been systematically studied is:

> End-to-end *roundtrip*-aligned training where the optimization target is **post–int8 + zlib decompression performance** (val_bpb), *under a strict serialized artifact size cap* (e.g., 16MB) and a tight runtime overhead constraint (≤1.10× baseline) for training.

### Why this is not already covered

- **QAT + entropy-aware training**:  
  Prior work focuses on:
  - Learning quantization scales/zero-points and possibly weight clustering.  
  - Minimizing entropy proxies (e.g., L2 regularization pushing weights toward a codebook) but still at the level of *raw tensors*, not actual **byte-level compressor behavior**.

- **Compression-aware or coders-in-the-loop training**:
  - Some “neural network compression” papers use bespoke entropy coders (e.g., Huffman, arithmetic, ANS) and integrate differentiable surrogates of bitrate.  
  - However, standard general-purpose compressors (e.g., zlib/DEFLATE) are treated as fixed black boxes used *after* training, not as targets for staged QAT schedules.
  - There is no standard methodology in the literature for **“roundtrip-aware QAT”** that trains explicitly against *the exact pipeline*: `float32 → int8 → zlib → (disk, 16MB limit) → zlib-decompress → int8 inference`, nor for **val_bpb measured on data after that full loop**.

- **Gate-budget / quick-gate constraints**:
  - Efficiency work commonly constrains FLOPs, latency, or energy, but not a **runtime overhead ceiling relative to an existing training script** (≤1.10×) combined with a **tight serialized artifact limit** (16MB) as first-class constraints.
  - “Parameter Golf”–style optimization (jointly tuning pruning, quantization, coding, etc. as a search space under a gate budget) is very recent and under-explored, especially with **val_bpb** (bits‑per‑byte or bits‑per‑byte-per-token) as the key objective.

Thus, the novel angle is:

> **Staged, low-overhead QAT explicitly aligned to the *non-differentiable* int8 + zlib roundtrip, optimized for post-roundtrip val_bpb under a strict serialized artifact size cap (16MB) and quick-gate (≤1.10× runtime).**

Instead of treating compression as a post-processing step, we *co-design* the training schedule and quantization regime such that the model is *robust to* and *co-adapted with* the actual discrete codec and artifact constraints.

### Why timely now (2024–2026)

- **Shift to on-device and edge models**: There is a strong push to deploy language and sequence models on phones, browsers, and small servers with tight disk and memory budgets (tens of MB). End-to-end pipelines that directly optimize for a “shipping artifact” size and performance are increasingly important.
- **Maturity of QAT + mixed-precision tooling**: Libraries like PyTorch 2.x, bitsandbytes, and emerging parameter-efficient runtimes make it feasible to integrate QAT and compression loops into standard training scripts on a single GPU.
- **Recent compression-centric LLM work**: There is a wave of research framing language modeling as compression and evaluating in bits-per-byte (bpb) or bits-per-character (bpc), but almost all of it assumes float or standard quantization without tightly integrating generic compressors like zlib into training-time objectives.

1. **Dettmers, T., et al., 2024 (or 2023/24)** – Work on 8-bit/4-bit training and quantization of LLMs (e.g., QLoRA, bitsandbytes QAT-style methods)  
   - **Relevance**: Demonstrates that QAT and low-bit quantization can preserve performance for large models with modest overhead. However, they focus on inference accuracy/perplexity under fixed quantization schemes, *not* on end-to-end artifact size with generic compressors (zlib) nor on val_bpb after full roundtrip.
