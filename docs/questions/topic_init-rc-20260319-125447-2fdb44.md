Parameter Golf val_bpb minimization under quick-harness gate, with 16MB artifact compliance on promotion  
Domains: **machine-learning, efficient-training, model-compression**

## Novel Angle

**Core idea:**  
Develop an *automatic parameter-golf controller* (“autoRC”) that **jointly** optimizes (a) *validation bits-per-byte* (val_bpb) and (b) *deployment-time artifact size* (≤16MB), **under a strict “quick-harness” constraint**: every candidate model must be trainable + evaluable within a very small, fixed wall-clock and compute budget (e.g., ≤1 GPU-hour, ≤X training tokens) and pass a standardized harness that mimics practical CI/CD promotion.

This is *not* just “do model compression” or “do hyperparameter search”; the novelty is:

1. **Joint objective: val_bpb + artifact size + harness latency**
   - Existing work on compression (pruning, quantization, distillation) mostly:
     - Optimizes *accuracy* or *perplexity* at a given compression ratio, or
     - Minimizes model size/FLOPs given a small accuracy drop.
   - Here we:
     - Use **val_bpb** (bits per byte) as the *primary* quality metric (as in modern language modeling / compression literature)  
     - Explicitly enforce a **hard artifact size constraint (≤16MB)** at *promotion time* (deployment), and  
     - Enforce a **“quick-harness gate”**: each candidate must be trainable + evaluable in a *very short, fixed* budget, making the search procedure itself a first-class object of study.

2. **Parameter Golf as a *constrained trajectory* in parameter-count vs. val_bpb vs. artifact-size space**
   - “Parameter golf” is typically an *informal* notion: iteratively shrinking models and tweaking hyperparameters until performance is “good enough.”
   - We formalize this as:
     - A **sequence** of models with monotonically decreasing parameter count and/or artifact size,
     - Under a **budgeted evaluation harness** (quick-harness gate), and
     - With a **promotion rule**: only models satisfying val_bpb ≤ τ and artifact_size ≤ 16MB can be promoted.
   - The research target is an **auto-controller** that *learns or adapts* how to move along this trajectory efficiently, instead of hand-tuned schedules.

3. **Quick-harness gate as a first-class constraint**
   - NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR 2024–2026 show growing interest in:
     - **Resource-adaptive training**, **anytime models**, **test-time adaptation**, and **deployment-aware optimization**.
   - However, almost all works:
     - Assume relatively unconstrained offline training/finetuning,
     - Or treat evaluation cost as negligible.
   - In real MLOps / CI pipelines, the gating step is **extremely constrained**: a model must be:
     - Trained/finetuned quickly,
     - Evaluated on a fixed harness,
     - And either promoted or rejected.
   - This quick-harness gate has not been systematically integrated into **compression + training** algorithms as an explicit constraint.

4. **Timeliness (2024–2026)**
   - Trends:
     - Small, deployable LMs and on-device models (e.g., TinyLLaMA, Phi-2/3-style small models, on-device assistants).
     - Strong emphasis on **reproducible, lightweight artifacts** and **model cards** with size/latency constraints.
     - Increasing focus on **data/compute-efficient training** and **low-resource hyperparameter optimization**.
   - Recent developments that open an opportunity:
     - **Modern small transformer architectures** that can achieve competitive val_bpb with fewer parameters.
     - **Post-training quantization and low-rank adaptation (LoRA/DoRA)** that allow aggressive compression with minimal quality loss.
     - **Lightweight HPO / neural architecture search (NAS)** methods that can run under tight budgets.
   - This project leverages these to **co-design**:
     - Compression strategy,
     - Training regimen,
     - And promotion gate under a 16MB artifact constraint.

5. **How this differs from standard approaches**
   - Not standard compression: we are not just “compressing a trained model”; we are:
     - Integrating compression choices into the *training+selection loop* under a quick-harness budget.
   - Not standard HPO: search is:
     - Strongly constrained by a **tiny evaluation budget**,
     - Multi-objective (val_bpb + artifact size), and
     - Enforced by a “promotion gate” abstraction, which is more aligned with CI/CD than with typical HPO settings.
   - Not just “train a small LM”: we aim to **algorithmically learn** a parameter-golf trajectory (sequence of shrinking models and configs) that reliably yields a promoted model under tight constraints.
