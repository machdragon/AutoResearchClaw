# Research Plan: parameter-golf-autorc

## Topic
Parameter Golf val_bpb minimization under a quick-harness gate, with 16MB artifact compliance on promotion, in the context of:
- **machine-learning**
- **efficient-training**
- **model-compression**

Concretely: automatic, compute-efficient parameter search (“parameter golf”) for language models that:
1. Minimizes **validation bits-per-byte (val_bpb)**,  
2. Operates under a **quick-harness gate** (fast, lightweight evaluation loop), and  
3. Enforces a **hard 16MB artifact size cap** on deployable/promoted models.

---

## Novel Angle

### What this actually is
We interpret the project as:

> Design an *automatic* parameter-search / compression controller that **jointly** optimizes:
> - validation bits-per-byte (val_bpb) as the main quality metric,
> - wall-clock / FLOP budget via a “quick-harness” evaluation loop,
> - and a strict **≤16MB model artifact** constraint for promotion.

The system (“parameter-golf-autorc”) should automatically:
- explore quantization, pruning, low-rank adapters, and small architectural tweaks;
- use **short, cheap validation harnesses** (quick-harness gate) to prune bad candidates;
- only run expensive full validation on a few survivors;
- and produce a fully-compressed model ≤16MB that is competitive in val_bpb.

### Why this is *not* standard model compression / NAS

Existing lines of work cover pieces of this, but *not the joint setting*:

1. **Compression with strong quality metrics, but not quick-harness gating**
   - Post-training quantization, pruning, and distillation (e.g., GPTQ, AWQ, sparse GPT variants, TinyLlama, Phi-3-mini) aim at small models, but:
     - They typically use standard validation loops (full perplexity eval, full test sets).
     - They don’t formalize a *two-stage* evaluation regime: fast approximate gate → selective full eval.
     - They rarely treat *val_bpb* explicitly; they rely on perplexity or accuracy.

2. **Neural architecture search / hyperparameter tuning, but not under hard artifact caps**
   - NAS and hyperparameter optimization (HPO) methods (BOHB, ASHA, population-based training, differentiable NAS) optimize accuracy vs. compute, but:
     - They usually don’t enforce **hard model-size constraints** (e.g., “must fit in 16MB artifact”).
     - They optimize training cost, not *deployment artifact size*.
     - They seldom integrate compression decisions (quantization/pruning) into the search space in a *first-class* way.

3. **Efficient evaluation / early stopping, but not for compression decisions**
   - Successive halving, early stopping, and low-fidelity proxies are well-studied, but:
     - They are applied to *training configurations*, not to *compression pipelines* (where each configuration might be a different quantization + pruning + adapter combination).
     - There is little work on **calibrating quick-harness metrics** (tiny validation slices, short contexts, reduced vocab) to a stable downstream metric like val_bpb under tight size constraints.

4. **Artifact-size-aware work is nascent and narrow**
   - Some works on on-device models (e.g., mobile NLP, edge LMs) consider model size and latency, but:
     - They tend to evaluate on *task accuracy* and *latency*, not **val_bpb** (a more fine-grained, compression-friendly metric).
     - They don’t usually define a *strict* hard cap (like “16MB total artifact including weights + minimal runtime metadata”) as a gating condition in an automated search loop.

### Why this is timely (2024–2026)

Recent trends make this direction particularly relevant now:

1. **Rise of tiny LMs and on-device inference**
   - There is a surge in work on sub-100M parameter models for phones, browsers, and micro-servers, with strong interest in <20MB artifacts.
   - Open-source ecosystems (Ollama, GGUF, WebLLM, MLC-LLM) are converging on size-limited artifacts for distribution and caching.

2. **Val_bpb as a unifying, compression-friendly metric**
   - Bits-per-byte (or bits-per-character) has become a standard metric for language compression and tokenization research, and is tightly linked to entropy coding and storage.
   - As LMs are used for compression and log-encoding tasks, val_bpb is more directly relevant than perplexity.

3. **Auto-tuning and “self-optimizing” ML systems**
   - There is strong interest in *auto-configuration* and *auto-compression* (e.g., AutoAWQ, AutoGPTQ, automated LoRA search).
   - However, these tools are mostly heuristic and do not formalize quick-harness gating or hard artifact caps as first-class design goals.

### How this differs from standard approaches

- **Joint objective**: We treat **val_bpb under a 16MB cap** as the primary optimization target, not just accuracy or perplexity.
- **Two-stage evaluation**: We explicitly design and analyze a **quick-harness gate**—a low-fidelity but cheap proxy for val_bpb—to drive an *automatic* search over compression/architecture parameters.
