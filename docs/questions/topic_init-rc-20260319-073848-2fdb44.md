# Research Plan: parameter-golf-autorc

## Topic
Parameter Golf for **val_bpb minimization** under a **quick-harness gate** with **16MB artifact size compliance** on promotion, in the context of:
- machine learning
- efficient training
- model compression / deployment constraints

---

## Novel Angle

**Core idea:**  
Design and analyze *parameter golf* procedures that automatically search over *training-time* and *architecture-level* compression knobs (pruning, quantization, low-rank, KV-cache tricks, tokenizer changes, etc.) **subject to a strict 16MB artifact constraint at promotion time**, while using **val_bpb (validation bits-per-byte)** as the primary optimization signal and enforcing a **“quick-harness gate”**: a small, fast, standardized evaluation harness that must be passed in minutes on a single GPU before any promotion.

### What is “Parameter Golf” here?

- Treat the model + training recipe as a **parameterized object** with:
  - Architecture knobs: width, depth, attention heads, vocab size, embedding dims.
  - Compression knobs: sparsity ratio, quantization bit-width, low-rank factorization rank, weight tying choices, KV cache compression.
  - Training knobs: learning rate schedule, number of steps, data subsampling, mixed precision choices, distillation temperature, etc.
- Use a lightweight **AutoRC (automatic recipe construction)** controller that:
  - Proposes configurations under a strict **16MB artifact budget**.
  - Gets feedback from a **quick-harness gate** (fast val_bpb on a small but representative validation slice).
  - Iteratively refines configs to minimize val_bpb subject to the size constraint.

### Why is this *not* already well-studied?

1. **Joint optimization of val_bpb + hard artifact-size constraint is underexplored**  
   - There is work on:
     - General model compression (pruning/quantization/distillation).
     - Efficient LMs and small LMs.
     - Hyperparameter optimization / NAS.
   - But **most**:
     - Optimize *perplexity* or *accuracy*, not **val_bpb** as a first-class, deployment-oriented metric.
     - Consider *model size* as a soft regularizer or secondary objective, not a **hard 16MB promotion gate**.
     - Do not explicitly model *promotion-time artifacts* (weights + tokenizer + minimal runtime) as the object whose size must be ≤16MB.

2. **Quick-harness gate as a first-class design constraint**  
   - “Quick harness” evaluations (small validation sets, fast tests) are used informally in industry, but:
     - There is almost no **formal study** of how to design such harnesses so that:
       - They are cheap enough (minutes on one GPU).
       - They are predictive of *promotion* performance (full val_bpb on larger corpora).
     - There is no systematic exploration of **how to tune parameter-golf search so that it performs well under a quick-harness gate** (i.e., search guided by partial, noisy, small-slice val_bpb).

3. **Auto-Recipe Construction (AutoRC) under artifact constraints**  
   - NAS and HPO typically:
     - Assume large compute budgets.
     - Do not enforce a *deployment artifact* constraint (weights + tokenizer + minimal runtime).
   - Recent small-model work (e.g., TinyLlama, Phi-2/3, Qwen1.5-1.8B) focuses on **hand-crafted** recipes and architectures.
   - What’s missing:
     - A **systematic, automated search** for recipes that are **artifact-size-aware** and **val_bpb-optimized**, tuned for **very small footprints (≤16MB)**, and explicitly evaluated through a **quick-harness gate**.

4. **Underexplored: information-theoretic metric (bpb) + compression tradeoff at tiny scales**  
   - Bits-per-byte (or bits-per-character) is standard in language modeling research, but:
     - Most work uses bpc/bpb primarily on large models and large datasets, not under **tiny, hard size caps ~16MB**.
     - The tradeoff between **val_bpb** and **artifact size** at *tiny* scales (e.g., <20MB) is not well-characterized, especially with **automated recipe search**.

### Why is this timely (2024–2026)?

1. **On-device and edge LMs are exploding**  
   - There is strong interest in **tiny LMs** that can run:
     - In browsers (WebGPU, WASM).
     - On microcontrollers or low-end phones.
   - 16MB–50MB is a realistic upper limit for many edge deployment contexts.

2. **Recent work shows the power of small, well-trained LMs & compression**  
   - Strong small models (e.g., TinyLlama, Phi-2/3, Qwen1.5-1.8B) demonstrate that **recipe and data curation** matter more than raw scale.
   - Quantization and pruning methods have matured enough that **joint automated tuning** for very tight budgets is now feasible.

3. **Rise of AutoML-like controllers for training pipelines**  
   - There is renewed interest in **automated training recipe search** and **systematic evaluation harnesses** for LMs.
   - But a gap remains for **explicit artifact-size-aware AutoRC** that is:
     - Low-compute.
     - Tailored to tiny deployment targets.
